Ambient:

  • Rotate items – incomplete boxes on the top and in the front to sell first.
  • Place loose units on top or in front of full ones to prioritize selling them first.
  • Avoid throwing loose units behind cases.
  • Close gaps and pull cases forward as you go.
  • When dealing with products that come in pairs, handle the second box if you've done the first one.
  • Similarly, if someone has already handled the first box, avoid taking the second one to save time.
  • Pay attention to the barcodes and product names on Tins, Jars, and some boxes. Ensure that the product name faces the customer when placing it on the shelf.
  • Avoid overfilling. If the shelf is sufficiently stocked, place the excess product in the backstock.

Chill:

  • Rotate items, including juices, cheese, and yoghurts.
  • If the shelf has enough product and/or varying dates, backstock the new delivery to sell through.

Produce:

  • Keep ambient produce by aisle; fruit with tomatoes on one pallet, carrots, peppers etc on other
  • Rotate salads with older ones on top of new ones.
  • Collect loose units from underneath shelving.
